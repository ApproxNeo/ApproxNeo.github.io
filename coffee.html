Pro tips for making coffee at home!!
  • Know your grind sizes. Different coffee brewing contraptions require different coffee ground sizes in order to extract them perfectly. Grind sizes range from fine (eg. Turkish) to coarse (eg. French Press). If unsure about the size of grind your machine requires, ask your friendly local coffee shop!
  • Pay attention to temperature. The temperature at which you brew your coffee can cause your coffee beans to be over- or under-extracted.
  • Pay attention to your espresso pull time. If your espresso takes too long to pull, or is too fast, you may want to start over again. If the problem persists, check your machine.

Try this simple, yet flavourful latte recipe!


Requirements:
  • Your most reliable espresso machine
  • A steaming wand
  • Coffee grounds (if grinding at home, remember to use the right setting)
  • A pitcher
  • Your favourite coffee mug☕ (or tea, we don't gatekeep here!)
  • Your favourite dairy alternative milk🥛 (almond or oat is recommended)

The recipe

  1. Pour some milk into your pitcher, depending on your mug size. Make sure to leave a little space for foam.
  2. Dip the steaming wand into the pitcher, and begin steaming. Raise the tip of the wand to the top of the milk (still submerged), and aerate for a count of two⏲️.
  3. Lower the tip back into the milk, and let milk reach 64 degrees celcius.
  4. While waiting for the milk, use your trusty espresso machine and pull a shot (or two!) of espresso, depending on the amount of caffeine you require.
  5. When the milk and espresso are both ready, pour the shots into your mug.
  6. Make sure the milk's surface is smooth and not bubbly or too foamy. In the case of excess foam, scoop some out with a spoon and swirl your milk in its pitcher to get the bubbles out.
  7. Combine the milk with the espresso- when experienced enough, practise your latte art and create a simple heart❤️
  8. Enjoy your hot, creamy latte with some of these delicious chocolate chip cookies!

Coffee Origins
Origin Description
Columbia Columbian beans have a mellow acdity and strong caramel sweetness, with a nutty undertone.
Kenya Kenyan coffee has a rich taste, with a wine-like taste and has tones of fruit and berry. Recommended if you're a new coffee drinker!
Ethiopia Ethiopian coffees have floral, herbal and citrus tasting notes. It has mild acidity and is not too strong a body- making it a favourite of many.
Sumatra Sumatran coffee is known for its earthy tones, and its low acidity. Sumatra was actually one of the first few places to start growing coffee on a large scale!
Guatemala Guatemalan coffee (the nicest, this is non-negotiable) has chocolatey favour notes, and is notably sweet. It has a medium to full body.
