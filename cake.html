RECIPE

My favourite cake recipe is from Binging with Babish's video on Tres Leches Cake. It's basically a Chiffon Cake!

Below are the video and the summarized recipe (for those who don't want to watch the video):

Ingredients Quantity
  • Eggs
  • White Sugar
  • All Purpose Flour
  • Baking Powder
  • Salt
  • Milk
  • Vanilla Extract
  • Vegetable Oil
  • Canned Sweetened Condensed Milk
  • Canned Evaporated Milk
  • Whole Milk
  • Strawberries
  • Baking Soda
  • Heavy Whipping Cream
  • 10
  • Approx. 3 Cups
  • 9.75 Ounces
  • 3 tsp
  • 1/2 tsp
  • 4 2/3 Cups
  • 2.5 tsp
  • For greasing pans
  • 1 14 Ounce
  • 1 12 Ounce
  • 4 Ounces
  • Depends, garnished, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 tsp
  • 8 Ounces
Steps
1. Separate 10 egg whites from the egg yolks. Place the white into the bowl of the Stand Mixer. Set egg yolks aside.
2. Beat egg whites on high speed while adding 1 cup of sugar slowly and whisking it stiff peaks, creating a meringue. Used for folding the final cake batter.
3. In a separate bowl, beat egg yolks with 1 cup of sugar with the Stand Mixer on medium speed for 2 minutes till pale and ribbon-y.
4. In a large bowl, combine 9 3/4 Ounces of All Purpose Flour, 3 tsp of Baking Powder and a 1/2 tsp of Salt and whisk together and set aside.
5. Back to the egg yolk mixture, add 2 1/3 Cups of milk while mixing. Add 1 1/2 tsp of Vanilla Extract.
6. Then combine the dry ingredient in step 4 into the egg yolk mixture and mix until completely combined.
7. Scrap the egg yolk mixture bowl, to prevent clumps from forming, then add the meringue and fold gently. Carefully not to deflate the egg whites. Keep going until there are no streaks of egg whites remain.
8. Get 2 pans and lubricate with Vegetable Oil. Get a parchment paper, cut to fit the pan perfectly and place it into the pan and pushing it down.
9. Pour the batter halfway into the pan and spread nice and smooth over top and rectify any uneven fillings.
10. Bake both batter in the oven at 350 Degrees Fahrenheit.
11. Now to prepare the Dulce de Leche. In a medium saucepan goes 1 1/4 Cups of Sugar, 4 Cups of Milk, 1/4 tsp of Baking Soda. Whisk gently to combine over medium heat until it comes to a rolling boil. At that point, lower the heat and simmer for 1 1/2 hour, whisking frequently to prevent scorching and wait patiently for the fat to caramelize into a rich golden brown.
12. Strain it on a Fine Mesh Sieve, and cool completely for 1 hour.
13. At this point, the cakes are ready to be taken out of the oven. Test the cake by inserting a toothpick at the center. If it emerges clean, it is done. Let them cool in the pans for at least an hour.
14. Run a butter knife around the outside edge of the cake and invert on a cooling rack. Remove the parchment paper on the bottom of the cake and place cake back to the pan.
15. Now the Tres de Leches part of the cake. In a large bowl, 1 14 Ounce Can of Sweetened Condensed Milk, 1 12 Ounce Can of Evaporated Milk and 4 Ounces of Whole Milk and tiny whisk together until homogeneous.
16. Finally, it's time to form the cake. First, impale the cakes multiple times with a fork and begin ladelling the Tres Leches mixture over the top. Cover the cakes overnight for at least 6 hours.
17. Next day, make a simple whipped cream. In a Stand Mixer, combine 8 Ounces of Heavy Whipped Cream, 2 tsp of Sugar and a teaspoon of Vanilla Extract. Beat on high speed until you get nice stiff peaks.
18. Now for assembly, place 1 cake on a plate, spread the Dulce de Leche on the cake, now add the Whipped Cream and thinly spread over the Dulche de Leche.
19. Next, place the second cake onto the first cake (inverted). Cover the top with another layer of Whipped Cream, and finally garnish with thinly sliced Strawberries. Depends on how you want to garnish, I'd like to garnish the middle of the cake with thinly sliced Strawberries but you can follow Binging with Babish way of garnishing by layering thinly sliced Strawberries in a ring shape then pour some Dulce de Leche in the middle.
20. Done! Enjoy your Tres Leche cake!
